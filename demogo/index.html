Pathogen

Alternaria solani

Early blight is caused by the fungus Alternaria solani. It manifests as small, brown-to-black spots on older leaves. These spots enlarge and develop characteristic concentric rings, creating a "target" effect. Immediate intervention is required to prevent total defoliation.

eco

Copper Fungicides (Organic)

Apply liquid copper soap at first sign of disease. Ensure thorough coverage of both leaf surfaces. Repeat every 7-10 days.

cut

Sanitary Pruning

Prune and remove infected lower leaves. Sterilize tools between plants to prevent spores from spreading to healthy tissues.

science

Synthetic Fungicides

Use chlorothalonil or mancozeb-based fungicides. Apply every 7-14 days depending on weather conditions. Always follow label instructions.

warning

Safety Precautions

Wear protective equipment. Observe harvest intervals. Not suitable for organic certification.

water_drop

Water Management

Avoid overhead irrigation. Water at base of plants early in morning to allow foliage to dry quickly.

recycling

Crop Rotation

Rotate with non-solanaceous crops for at least 2 years. Remove all plant debris at season end.
